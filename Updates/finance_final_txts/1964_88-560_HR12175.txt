Approved September 2, 1964. Public Law 88-561 September 2, 1964 [H.R. 130] Reclamation projects. Rights-of-way. AN ACT To provide for the payment of compensation, including severance damages, for rights-of-way acquired by the United States in connection with reclamation projects the construction of which commenced after January 1, 1961. Be it enacted hy the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled, That notwithstand- ing the existence of any reservation of right-of-way for canals under the Act of August 30, 1890 (26 Stat. 371, 391; 43 U.S.C. 945), the Secretary of the Interior shall pay just compensation, including severance damages, to the owners of private land utilized for ditches or canals in connection with any reclamation project, or any unit or any division of a reclamation project, provided the construction of said ditches or canals commenced after January 1, 1961, and such compensation shall be paid notwithstanding the execution of any agreements or any judgments entered in any condemnation proceed- ing, prior to the effective date of this Act. Approved September 2, 1964. Public Law 88-562 September 2, 1964 [H.R. 11338] Massachusetts Port Authority. Land transfer, repeal of con- ditions. AN ACT To remove certain conditions subject to which certain real property in South Boston, Massachusetts, was authorized to be conveyed to the Massachusetts Port Authority. Be it enacted hy the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled, That section 2 of the Act entitled \"Aii Act to authorize the Secretary of the Navy to trans- fer to the Massachusetts Port Authority, an instrumentality of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, certain lands and improvements thereon comprising a portion of the so-called E Street Annex, South Boston Annex, Boston Naval Shipyard, in South Boston, Massachu- setts, in exchange for certain other lands\", approved July 7, 1960 (Public Law 86-602; 74 Stat. 355), is repealed. Approved September 2, 1964."
