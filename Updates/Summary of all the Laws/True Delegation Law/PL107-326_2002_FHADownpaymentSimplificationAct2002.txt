S.2239 
Title: A bill to amend the National Housing Act to simplify the downpayment requirements for
FHA mortgage insurance for single family homebuyers. 
Sponsor: Sen Sarbanes, Paul S. [MD] (introduced 4/24/2002)      Cosponsors (24) 
Related Bills: H.R.858, H.R.3661 
Latest Major Action: Became Public Law No: 107-326 [GPO: Text, PDF]
SUMMARY AS OF: 
10/17/2002--Passed Senate amended.    (There are 2 other summaries)
FHA Downpayment Simplification Act of 2002 - Amends the National Housing Act to make the
existing Federal Home Administration (FHA) single-family downpayment provisions permanent.
(Such provisions are to expire at the end of 2002.)
Requires an original lender, in conjunction with a FDA insured loan, to provide a prospective
borrower with a one-page analysis of other mortgage products for which he or she would qualify,
including information about: (1) rates, insurance premiums, and other costs and fees; and (2)
mortgage insurance premium termination.
Amends the Higher Education Amendments of 1998 to repeal the Government National Mortgage
Association (GNMA) three-percent guarantee fee increase scheduled to take effect in FY 2005.
Amends the National Housing Act to provide for indexing of multi-family mortgage limits for FHA
mortgage insurance programs.
