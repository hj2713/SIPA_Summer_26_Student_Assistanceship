# Prompt Version 7: Staged Coding of Delegation, Constraints, and Discretion (Professor Coding Sheet Alignment)

You are coding a law for the level of discretionary authority delegated to administrative actors, following the precise variables defined in the research coding sheet.

Complete each stage in order. Provide the required output for each stage before moving to the next stage. Use the outputs from earlier stages to inform later stages.

---

## General Coding Instructions

Code only the text supplied. Do not infer authority or constraints from background law unless the supplied text expressly incorporates it.

### Definitions for purposes of this coding:
* **Delegation** means the law gives an agency or administrative actor authority or responsibility to act.
* **Delegees**: The specific agency, commission, board, department, or administrative actor receiving authority.
* **Discretion** means the agency has room to choose policy, standards, enforcement priorities, exemptions, interpretations, approvals, waivers, supervision methods, or implementation details.
* **Constraint** means the law limits, channels, reviews, conditions, or structures the agency’s use of delegated authority.

For boolean indicators below, assign **Y** (Yes) if the feature is present in the text, or **N** (No) if it is absent or not mentioned.

## Stage 3: Summary and Overall Discretion Classification
Synthesize the delegation level and the density of constraints identified in the prior stages to assign the overall discretion rank.

### Discretion Rank Scale (0–4)
* **0 = No discretion**: The law does not delegate any authority.
* **1 = Minimal discretion**: Low delegation, or the delegated task is narrow, mechanical, procedural, or ministerial. Highly constrained.
* **2 = Limited discretion**: Bounded authority. The agency has regulatory/enforcement powers, but they are checked by significant procedural or political constraints (e.g. reports, hearings, time limits, appeals, consultation).
* **3 = Substantial discretion**: Meaningful authority to make rules, supervise, regulate, or set standards. Constraints exist but leave large room for agency judgment.
* **4 = High discretion**: Broad standard-setting, rulemaking, or policymaking authority with few or no meaningful constraints.

### Logical Consistency Rules
* If `DelegateLaw` is **N**, `Discretion_Rank` must be **0**.
* If `Discretion_Rank` is **4**, constraints must be very low or absent. If multiple constraints (like time limits, legislative approval, and consultation) are present, the score must be lower (typically 2 or 3).

* **Final_Rationale**: [1-2 sentences explaining how the presence/absence of specific constraints leads to the final score]
