- Verify the completed sample size for every model-strategy row from the execution logs.
- Add an N column to Table V.
- Change the title to:

"Cross-model evaluation on the expert-coded delegated-law subset. Completed N varies by model and strategy; the maximum benchmark size is 161."

- Report persistent failures or incomplete runs in the text or a table note.
- Do not claim that one strategy improves on another unless the comparison uses the same completed cases. If common-case results are available, report them. If not, qualify the comparison.

2. REPLACE THE RESULTS INTERPRETATION IN SECTION VII.C

Delete or revise the statement that the Sequential Ordinal Cascade and Direct Multiclass strategies “consistently outperform” the One-Shot Baseline across models. The One-Shot Baseline is reported only for Gemini 3.5.

Replace it with:

"For Gemini 3.5, Direct Multiclass Rank Prediction reports the strongest aggregate results among the reported configurations. Exact-match accuracy increases from 28.6% for the unconstrained One-Shot Baseline to 41.9%, while within-one accuracy rises from 62.1% to 76.9% and mean absolute error falls from 1.250 to 0.915."


Delete the paragraph claiming that more than 85% of errors are off by one rank and fewer than 15% are multi-rank errors. The percentages reported in Table V do not support those claims.

Replace it with:

"Within-one accuracy exceeds exact-match accuracy across all configurations, indicating that many errors are adjacent. However, errors of two or more ranks remain non-de minimis for several model-strategy combinations, and weighted kappa remains low. Overall, the models show partial ordinal agreement with the historical expert-coded benchmark."
