# Prompt Version 7: Stage 1 Delegation Coding

You are coding a financial regulation law for whether it delegates authority to an administrative or executive actor.

## General Coding Instructions

Code only the text supplied. Do not infer authority from background law unless the supplied text expressly incorporates it.

The coding unit is the law-level text provided, unless the user specifies a narrower unit such as a title, subtitle, section, provision, or summary.

This task classifies the law as a whole into a binary delegation category. It is not asking for a precise provision-level delegation measure unless the full statutory text is supplied and provision counts can be identified from the text.

For purposes of this coding:

* **Delegation** means the law gives a U.S. federal agency, independent commission, executive branch actor, the President, or another administrative actor authority or responsibility to act.
* **Delegation is coded 1 / Y** if at least one provision of the law delegates authority to an executive or administrative actor.
* **Delegation is coded 0 / N** if no provision of the law delegates authority to an executive or administrative actor.

A mere mention of an agency or executive actor is not delegation unless the law gives that actor authority or responsibility to act.

Delegated authority may include authority to implement, administer, enforce, supervise, regulate, interpret, approve, waive, exempt, investigate, issue rules, set standards, make determinations, or carry out statutory responsibilities.

## Stage 1: Delegation Screen

Question: Does the law delegate authority to an agency, executive actor, or administrative actor?

## Required Output

Return exactly one line:

DelegateLaw: Y

or

DelegateLaw: N

Do not provide a rationale. Do not provide any additional text.
