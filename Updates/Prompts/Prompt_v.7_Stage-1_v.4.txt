# Prompt Version 7: Stage 1 Delegation Coding

You are coding a financial regulation law for whether it delegates authority to an administrative or executive actor.


## General Coding Instructions

Code only the text supplied. Do not infer authority from background law unless the supplied text expressly incorporates it.

The coding unit is the law-level text provided, unless the user specifies a narrower unit such as a title, subtitle, section, provision, or summary.


This task classifies the law as a whole into a binary delegation category. It is not asking for a precise provision-level delegation measure unless the full statutory text is supplied and provision counts can be identified from the text.

## Scope Rule for Financial Regulation and Omnibus Laws

Some laws are omnibus laws and contain titles, subtitles, sections, or provisions that address subjects outside financial regulation.

For this coding task, analyze only the parts of the supplied text that concern financial regulation.

Financial regulation includes laws or provisions concerning:

1. securities markets, securities exchanges, broker-dealers, investment advisers, investment companies, public companies, disclosure, registration, or investor protection;
2. commodities markets, commodity exchanges, futures, swaps, derivatives, or the CFTC;
3. banking, bank holding companies, national banks, state banks, thrifts, credit unions, deposit insurance, bank supervision, or bank regulators;
5. consumer credit, consumer financial protection, lending, credit reporting, debt collection, or payment systems;
6. financial institutions, financial markets, financial stability, systemic risk, capital requirements, liquidity requirements, prudential supervision, or resolution authority;
7. insurance, when the provision regulates insurance markets, insurance companies, financial risk, solvency, or related financial activity;
8. Treasury, Federal Reserve, SEC, CFTC, FDIC, OCC, CFPB, NCUA, or other financial regulators acting in a regulatory, supervisory, enforcement, or administrative capacity.

Do not code delegation found only in unrelated parts of the law, such as agriculture, health, education, defense, transportation, immigration, labor, housing, and urban development, or other non-financial topics.

If the law contains both financial and non-financial titles, base `DelegateLaw` only on the financial-regulation portions of the text.

If the supplied text does not make clear which provisions are financial-regulation provisions, apply the rule using only the information available in the supplied text.


For purposes of this coding:

* **Delegation** means the law gives a U.S. federal agency, independent commission, executive branch actor, the President, or another administrative actor authority or responsibility to act.
* **Delegation is coded 1 / Y** if at least one provision of the law delegates authority to an executive or administrative actor.
* **Delegation is coded 0 / N** if no provision of the law delegates authority to an executive or administrative actor.

A mere mention of an agency or executive actor is not delegation unless the law gives that actor authority or responsibility to act.

Delegated authority may include authority to implement, administer, enforce, supervise, regulate, interpret, approve, waive, exempt, investigate, issue rules, set standards, make determinations, or carry out statutory responsibilities.

## Stage 1: Delegation Screen

Question: Does the law delegate authority to an agency, executive actor, or administrative actor?

## Required Output

Return exactly one line:

DelegateLaw: Y

or

DelegateLaw: N

Do not provide a rationale. Do not provide any additional text.
