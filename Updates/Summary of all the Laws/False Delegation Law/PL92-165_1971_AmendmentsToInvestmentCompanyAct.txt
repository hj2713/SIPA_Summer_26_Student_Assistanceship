Mutual Funds
An article from CQ Almanac 1971
Congress Nov. 15 completed action on a bill (S 2216—PL 92 - 165 ) amending the Investment Company Act
amendments of 1970 (PL 91-547) to provide certain refund rights for persons buying mutual fund shares.
Final action came when the House by voice vote passed HR 9758 (H Rept 92 -623) and then approved the
identical Senate bill, S 2216, in its place.
The Senate had approved the bill Aug. 6 by voice vote. It had been reported from the Senate Banking, Housing and
Urban Affairs Committee Aug. 3 (S Rept 92-344).
As enacted the bill restricted the refund rights for mutual funds provided in PL 91-547 to participants in contract
investment plans under which sales charges exceeding 9 percent of any payment were deducted.
In the 1970 Act, Congress provided refund rights for persons buying mutual fund shares under periodic payment
contracts. These were intended to apply to “front-end load” contracts under which sales charges were concentrated
in the early months and years of a contract plan. Large deductions for sales charges in the early life of such plans
reduced the amount of an investor's payments which actually were invested. (1970 Almanac p. 890)
The Act required a mutual fund to send to each new investor, within 60 days after purchase of a contract plan, a
notice of the investor's right to cancel the plan. The Act entitled an investor to a refund of all sales charges plus the
value of his share account if he chose to cancel the plan within 45 days after receiving the notice.
The language of the Act actually applied the refund right to all contract plans, including mutual fund and variable
annuity plans under which only one payment and one sales charge deduction were made and those under which
the sales charge was spread evenly over the life of the plan.
Because of the wording of the Act, the Securities and Exchange Commission was unable to grant exemption to
single-payment and level-load plans. S 2216 limited the refund provision to plans under which sales charges
exceeding 9 percent of any payment were deducted.
