Securities Exemption
An article from CQ Almanac 1970
President Nixon Dec. 19 signed into law a bill (S 336—PL 91 - 565 )
increasing to $500,000 from $300,000 the amount of a small business
securities issue which the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) was
permitted to exempt from certain registration requirements.
Final Congressional action came Dec. 7 when the House passed the bill by
voice vote. The Senate had passed it Aug. 13 by voice vote.
Provision. As cleared by Congress, S 336 raised the amount of a securities
issue which the SEC could by regulation exempt from registration
requirements to $500,000 from $300,000.
Background. The Securities Exchange Act of 1933, as amended in 1945,
permitted the SEC to exempt securities issues up to $300,000 from the more
costly and time-consuming requirements established for larger issues. The
aim was to assist small businesses which needed to raise capital; the
registration procedure established involved only SEC regional offices.
Legislative History. The bill was reported Aug. 10 by the Senate Banking
and Currency Committee (S Rept 91 -1082) and passed by the Senate
Aug. 13 on a voice vote. (Hearings, box p. 914)
The House Interstate and Foreign Commerce Committee reported S 336 (H
Rept 91-1654) on Dec. 2 and it was passed by the House Dec. 7, also by
voice vote.
