Question

Based on the delegated authority and constraints identified in earlier analysis, classify the law as a whole by the level of discretionary authority it grants to administrative actors.

This is a law-level Rough Guide classification, not a precise provision-level discretion measure.

Discretion Rank Scale
0 = No Discretion

The law does not delegate authority to an agency or administrative actor.

1 = Minimal Discretion

The law delegates only a small amount of authority, or the agency's role is narrow, mechanical, procedural, or ministerial. Delegated authority is tightly constrained.

2 = Limited Discretion

The law delegates some real implementation, supervisory, regulatory, or enforcement authority, but the authority is bounded by significant rules, standards, reporting, consultation, appeals, exemptions, oversight, deadlines, or other constraints.

3 = Substantial Discretion

The law delegates meaningful authority to interpret, implement, enforce, supervise, regulate, approve, waive, exempt, or set standards. Constraints exist, but agencies retain significant room to exercise judgment.

4 = High Discretion

The law delegates broad policymaking, rulemaking, standard-setting, waiver, exemption, enforcement, supervisory, or interpretive authority across many provisions or central parts of the law, with few meaningful constraints on how that authority is used.

Factors to Consider When Assigning the Rank

Evaluate:

Breadth of affected actors, markets, institutions, transactions, or activities.
Degree of agency policy choice.
Strength of statutory constraints.
Centrality of agency implementation to the law.
Consistency Rules
Rank 0

Assign if:

No delegation exists.
DelegateLaw = N.
Rank 1

Assign if:

Delegation is low.
Agency role is mostly ministerial, procedural, mechanical, or narrow.
Delegated authority is heavily constrained.
Rank 2

Assign if:

Agency receives meaningful implementation, supervisory, regulatory, or enforcement authority.
Significant constraints limit how authority can be exercised.
Rank 3

Assign if:

Agency receives substantial authority to interpret, implement, enforce, supervise, regulate, approve, waive, exempt, or set standards.
Constraints exist but still leave significant room for agency judgment.
Rank 4

Assign only if:

Delegation is broad, extensive, or central to the law.
Agency retains major policy-making or standard-setting discretion.
Constraints are limited, weak, or minimal.
Broad authority extends across many provisions or central parts of the law.

Do not assign Rank 4 merely because authority is delegated.
