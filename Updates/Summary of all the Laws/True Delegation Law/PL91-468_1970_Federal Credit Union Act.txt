Credit  Union Insurance
An article from CQ Almanac 1970
Congress Oct. 5 cleared for the President a bill (S 3822—PL 91-468) establishing a Federal insurance  system
for state and Federally chartered credit unions .
Final action came when the House passed S 3822 by voice vote. The Senate had passed the bill Sept. 2 by voice
vote.
S 3822 required Federally chartered credit unions  to insure their accounts and allowed state chartered
groups to join the program. Deposits of up to $20,000 would be insured.
Provisions. As cleared by Congress, S 3822:
Required that the administrator of the National Credit  Union Administration insure the accounts of all Federally 
chartered credit unions , and authorized him to insure state- chartered credit unions  which apply for coverage and
which maintain the same reserves as Federally chartered credit unions . All Federally chartered credit unions
would be given one year in which to qualify for insurance ; after that time, the charters of noncomplying credit
unions  would be revoked or suspended.
Set annual premiums at one-twelfth of 1 percent of total accounts. When the fund reached its normal operating level of
1 percent of member accounts, the administrator could reduce the premiums. If expenditures exceeded the fund's
income for a year, the administrator could raise the premium for that year up to one-sixth of 1 percent.
Allowed credit unions  serving predominantly low-income members (as defined by the administrator) to accept
funds from nonmembers.
Established a revolving fund in the Treasury for administering and operating the program.
Senate
Committee Action. The Senate Banking and Currency Committee Aug. 19 reported S 3822 (S Rept 91-1128)
establishing a Federal insurance  system for credit unions .
As reported, the provisions of the bill were the same as those that cleared Congress.
The report said, “Only a very small proportion of the more than $14 billion of savings held by 24,000 credit unions
 operating in the United States is insured against loss by the saver.” It added that most credit  union
members did not realize their savings were uninsured. The bill would protect savings up to $20,000.
The provision allowing credit unions  serving low-income persons to receive capital from outside sources would
help provide loans at reasonable rates of interest for poor members, the report stated.
Floor Action. The Senate Sept. 2 by voice vote and with little debate passed S 3822. As passed, the provisions of
the bill were the same as those that cleared Congress. The bill then went to the House.
House
Committee Action. The House Banking and Currency Committee Sept. 21 reported without amendment the
Senate-passed bill (S 3822—H Rept 91–1457) establishing a Federal insurance  system for state and Federally 
chartered credit unions .

passed by the Senate Sept. 2. The bill was adequate to begin such a program, it said, and the program could be

bill for the President's signature.
